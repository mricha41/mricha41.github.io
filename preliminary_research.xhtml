Wireless Doorbell System – Analytical Results

General Information

All participants gave their verbal consent to participate in this research. A total of four participants were recruited, all of whom participated in both the interview and task analysis portions of the research. Because of the limited number of participants, only qualitative results will be provided. Some biases in the results may have arisen due to the constraints on time and the limited availability of participants. Participants were also required to respond using their phones for all tasks, which may or may not be the primary mode of response. It is assumed for the purposes of research, and the viability of wireless doorbell system products in general, that it is the primary mode of response. Collecting data via a questionnaire would give more detail regarding the user experience that is not readily obtained through observation and interview.

Short structured interviews were performed with wireless doorbell system users using the following questions:

  • •How often are you at home? 

  • •What is your daily routine? 

  • •What features of your wireless doorbell system do you use? 

 

Through these structured interviews with wireless doorbell system users, three distinct classes of users have been identified according to the following defining characteristics:

  • •feature set use 

  • •frequency of presence at the home 

 

These user classes are:

  • •Sophisticated Shut-in 

  • •Practical Busybody 

  • •Sophisticated Socialite 

 

Sophisticated Shut-in users are home all the time and exhibit the behaviors you might expect from technologically adept users in general. They use many of the features wireless doorbell systems have to offer and usually have a lot to say about them and share their overall vision of the product. Incidentally, they are aware of the competition and tend to favor a broad feature set. They may utilize a wireless doorbell system for surveillance, communication, and other more advanced features rather than run-of-the-mill smart phone alerts.

 

Practical Busybody users are home all the time, focused on their work, and tend to use a more restricted set of wireless doorbell system features. They prefer to maintain their productivity while keeping a close eye on smart phone alerts, prioritizing their responses depending upon the nature of the notification. They are less aware of the features available and may favor value over technical sophistication. Getting buy-in for advanced features may be challenging with this class of users.

 

Sophisticated Socialite users are home less often, but also exhibit the behaviors you might expect from the technologically adept. Similarly to Sophisticated Shut-in users, they use many of the features wireless doorbell systems have to offer and usually have a lot to say about them and share their overall vision of the product. Surveillance is more important to these users as well as communication features since they are home less often, but they also show interest in other advanced features.

Task Analysis

 

Each user was asked to perform the following actions with their wireless doorbell system:

  • •view the video live feed 

  • •respond to a typical doorbell smart phone alert (prompted by the researcher) 

  • •use two-way audio (prompted by the researcher) 

 

A more complete analysis would have included setup of the device. However, since this research was performed on-site, setup had already taken place and could not be studied. In addition, since most of the participants did not have the necessary subscriptions for video storage, tasks revolving around this feature were not included. This is unfortunate, because some interesting features of both the Nest and the Ring depend on the recording and processing of video footage.

 

Participants were generally able to perform each task quickly and without problems. Because each participant had different levels of experience with their devices, some bias may be present. However, since the tasks were largely performed using similar steps, the results are generally reliable.

 

When participants were asked to view the live feed of their wireless doorbell system, the sub-tasks were generally the same:

  • •participant launches the smart phone app 

  • •the participant is presented with a device or camera list 

  • •the participant selects the device or camera from the list 

  • •the participant views the video feed 

 

 For the smart phone alert task, participants followed the same steps to complete the task:

  • •researcher rings doorbell 

  • •participant receives alert 

  • •participant swipes/touches to respond to alert 

  • •participant views video feed 

 

For the two-way audio task, participants were prompted by the researcher to demonstrate the functionality by responding to a smart phone alert in their usual manner. The series of steps varied somewhat, but was mostly the same. The Google Nest has prerecorded responses available, which one participant used. Because two out of four respondents had the Nest and one responded similarly to the other two participants with different hardware, the essential steps are as follows:

  • •researcher rings doorbell 

  • •participant receives the alert 

  • •participant swipes/touches to respond to alert 

  • •participant views video feed 

  • •participant responds to the alert
